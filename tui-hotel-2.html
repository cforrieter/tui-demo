Sensimar Elounda Village Resort & Spa  

  • Overview

Late breakfasts, pillow menus, and sundowner cocktails are all part of the Sensimar Elounda Village Resort & Spa experience.

This adults-only hotel is set up high on a clifftop, edged by greenery, overlooking Mirabello Bay.

Bedrooms come with little luxuries like pillow menus and bathrobes. For something extra special, you can upgrade to a bungalow with a private pool.

Facilities ► View all

3 outdoor pools

Three good-sized pools that are all interconnected with waterfalls. With sunbeds, towels and parasols.

Buffet restaurant

For buffet breakfast, with the option of early or late breakfasts and a buffet dinner. Just to let you know there's a dress code here - men must wear full-length trousers for dinner.

Lobby bar*

With indoor and outdoor seating, offering views of the wide open-sea and the lights shining in the distance. For local drinks, please be aware that any imported drinks may have a higher charge. Just so you know, gentlemen must wear full-length trousers in the evenings.

Rooms► View all

junior suite with sea view and balcony or terrace

These junior suites come with a sea view, and either a balcony or terrace, offering that extra space outside. Your room will include a double bed, sofa bed, minibar*, bathrobes and slippers, and air-conditioning.

bungalow with garden view and balcony or terrace

These bungalows are a great base for exploring the surrounding area, and they come with a garden view and either a balcony or terrace, offering that extra space outside. They include a double bedroom, an extra bed, bathrobes and slippers, a minibar*, Wi-Fi*, and air-conditioning.

Amazing holiday and can't wait to return

21 Feb , 2016

This is a special place for us as we got engaged whilst staying here in September. It was an obvious choice for our honeymoon and we cant wait to return in June. Food and entertainment: We stayed all-inclusive and the only time we veered from this was to celebrate with Champagne on the evening I said yes! We preferred to dine at the a la carte restaurants most nights, and booking wasnt a problem. Lunch (and the odd cocktail) at the Beach Bar was our favourite option. Service was always friendly but not intrusive. There was a huge choice and never a queue when it came to breakfast. The Greek Night was excellent fun. Room: Our room was beautiful, with a well replenished fridge and toiletries and a good sized bath and shower. We had a car park view which went into the shade towards the afternoon, but that just meant we spent more time by the pools. Other amenities: Hills/steps I did have one moment when I had to take my heels off to walk to the Beach Bar, but that wasnt an issue and in flats its no problem. As we were there for a week we didnt plan any trips, but in June are looking forward to trips to Knossos, Spinalonga and nearby towns. Overall we would definitely recommend this hotel; the accommodation, atmosphere, service and entertainment were all of a high standard and worth the price.More

Even better than it looks!!

07 Feb , 2016

When we first arrived at the hotel we couldn't believe that it was the hotel advertised - the hotel its self was huge and so beautiful, we were expecting it to be nice but it was so much better. The transfer from the airport isn't too bad.We arrived at midnight and were greeted with champagne and quite a few members of staff. At check in we were told that our room was not ready, so they had upgraded us for one night/two days into a room with a private pool, this was great for us! The room was spacious, clean with a bottle of free wine and water. The bathroom was an open bathroom, with a slidey wall/door which you could close to separate it from the bedroom, this room was all very open, not great if you are want privacy but for us it was not a problem. The pool was beautiful too with a lovely view. On the second night we were moved to our garden view room where we spent the rest of the holiday. The room itself was bigger than the first room, with the bathroom separate. The patio doors lead out into a cute little patio with beautiful flowers in the garden. The rooms were all so clean and neat, i couldn't fault them. The hotel is based on the side of a cliff, so beautiful, but the only one thing that i could fault (which is being very picky), was the walk from the beach and sea area to the top reception main area of the hotel, (however this is only because im lazy!) i would say that this isnt a good hotel if you do have problems with mobility and walking. However i do think it is wheelchair friendly, and i did see some people getting taken around in little carts - but there is a lot of steep hills and walking! The main reception area and food areas are at the top of the hill, the pool is three-tiered at different points down the hill, and a beach and sea area at the bottom. Our favourite part to sunbathe were the sunbeds on the decking near the sea, which were like double mattresses. The hotel is a long walk to the village of Elounda and although we did walk it once i would definitely not do it again, taxi's are not that expensive! There was never a shortage of sunbeds in the hotel, which such large pool and beach areas there was no need to reserve your beds early. All around these areas are beautiful views. The food was a mixture of all sorts, as a picky eater i was very happy with the selection! In the main restaurant you could eat either in or out doors. One night we ate in the al a carte restaurant which was great, we sat outdoor with great entertainment. There is a small restaurant outside, mid level, with amazing views which we ate at for lunch one day. There is also a bar area, where you can also get food outside, as well as drinks and ice-creams. When you get food and drinks you are not charged anything, it is simply put onto your tab, you must sign for everything you get, this bill is paid upon checking out. The staff at the hotel were all sooo friendly and happy. Always there to help. The only bad part of the holiday was the transfer through thomson back to the airport - when i saw the time that it was leaving the hotel i was concerned that it was not early enough but was reassured by a thomson rep we would be there in plently of time for shopping and food, this was not the case and we arrived 30 mins before our plane was due to take off, the screen said our flight was boarding when we arrived, and we had still yet to queue for check-in. Overall we had a great week and truely couldn't fault the hotel, it was beautiful. Would definitely recommend!!More
